Mean-reversion dissents (~$60,693) — the bull case

ELI22 One model says 'relax — it already fell too far below where it should be, so it'll bounce back toward ~$60,693 instead of dropping more.' That's the hopeful take.

It ignores the cycle entirely and tracks Bitcoin's decade-long growth trend. It says the long-run floor is ~$78,531 and fair value ~$153,123 — implying spot ($59,943) is actually below trend.
ELI22 This one doesn't care about the cycle at all — it follows Bitcoin's 10-year growth path. It says long-term the floor is ~$78,531 and Bitcoin's actually cheap right now. Different question, much longer horizon.
Monte Carlo is the honest forecast: median $38,960, 80% band $26,029–$55,817
Instead of one line, it rolls realistic randomness and crash-jumps thousands of times. Half of all outcomes land near the middle; the band shows how wide the genuine uncertainty is.
ELI22 Instead of one prediction we rolled the dice 120,000 times with realistic crashes baked in. Middle guess $38,960, but it could plausibly be anywhere from $26,029 to $55,817. The width is the point.
Big caveat: n=10 data points
The R²=0.9+ on LPPL/damped (starred*) is overfitting — those models have nearly as many knobs as points at this resolution. Trust the cluster and the Monte-Carlo band, not any single decimal.
ELI22 We have 10 dots. A model that 'fits perfectly' is just memorizing the dots, not understanding them — like acing a test off the answer key. Trust the rough range and the dice-roll spread, never an exact number.

What this tells you (2,000 Monte-Carlo paths · project composite classifier · 14-bar window)

Down-regimes lead, but it's closer to a coin-flip with a fat bounce tail (~54% down vs ~33% up at Oct 31)
Summing the bearish labels gives ~54%, bullish ~33%, and ranging_volatile ~14%. The decline is the base case, not a certainty — a quarter of paths are net-up at the bottom date.
ELI22 Out of 2,000 imagined futures, about 54 in 100 are falling at the bottom date and 33 in 100 are actually rising. Down wins, but it's nowhere near a sure thing.
The dominant regime is trending_down_choppy (~37%) — this corrects the earlier ranging_directional guess
My hand-analysis expected ranging_directional_down to dominate; the classifier says it's rare (~4%) and the real workhorse is trending_down_choppy (~37%). So the ratchet lever that matters is the choppy-trend tier (currently 2.0× ATR), not the directional tier — widening ranging_directional_down would barely move the needle.
ELI22 I guessed wrong earlier. The market spends most of its time in 'falling but messy' (~37%), almost never in the specific 'stealth-trend' bucket I named (~4%). So tune the dial that's actually used, not the one I assumed.
~26% of the time it's trending_up_choppy — frequent counter-trend bounces
Even inside a net decline, ~26% of windows read as choppy-up. For a short, those are exactly the squeezes that trip a too-tight trailing stop — which is why a 1.0× trail bleeds out by a thousand cuts.
ELI22 Even on the way down, the price keeps throwing fake-out rallies about 26% of the time. If your stop is too tight, those head-fakes keep kicking you out right before it drops again.
The regime mix barely changes Jul → Nov — the bottom is not its own regime here
Because the predicted drift (~−5.7%/mo) is small versus daily volatility, the month-to-month odds are nearly flat. The classifier sees no distinct 'capitulation' signature in an iid bootstrap — the turn is a price event, not a separate regime the model surfaces.
ELI22 The 'mood odds' look almost identical every month. The predicted fall is too gentle compared to daily noise for the model to flag a special 'bottom' moment — it just looks like more of the same chop.
Caveat: this depends on the 14-bar window and an iid bootstrap
A longer regime window would shift mass toward ranging/directional labels; real markets also cluster volatility (GARCH), which would create more persistent volatile/trending streaks than independent resampling produces. Treat the ordering of regimes as robust, the exact percentages as soft.
ELI22 We used a 2-week lookback and shuffled past days independently. Real markets bunch their wild days together, so true life would have longer calm and crazy streaks. Trust the ranking of moods, not the exact numbers.

Model formulas & fitting algorithms (t = months since the Oct 6 2025 peak; coefficients per timeframe are in each tab's table)

1. Linear — ordinary least squares
P(t) = a + b·t
Fit: least squares on (t, P): minimize Σᵢ (Pᵢ − a − b·tᵢ)² (numpy.polyfit, degree 1). b is the $/month slope.
Projects: evaluate a + b·t at each future date.
2. Exponential — log-linear OLS
P(t) = P₀ · e^(k·t) ⇔ ln P(t) = ln P₀ + k·t
Fit: OLS of ln P on t; k = monthly log-decline rate, reported as (e^k − 1)·100 %/mo.
Projects: P₀ · e^(k·t).
3. Power law — long-run, published coefficients
log₁₀ P = 5.82 · log₁₀(d) − 17.01 (support line: −17.30); d = days since genesis (2009-01-03)
Fit: NOT fit to this decline — published Santostasi-style coefficients, evaluated at the bottom date's d.
Projects: P = 10^(5.82·log₁₀ d − 17.01) for the central line; the −17.30 intercept gives the support floor.
4. Damped oscillation — on log-price
ln P(t) = c₀ + c₁·t + e^(−d·t) · [ c₂·cos(ω·t) + c₃·sin(ω·t) ]
Fit: grid search over damping d and angular frequency ω; for each (d, ω) the linear coefficients (c₀, c₁, c₂, c₃) are solved by least squares (cos/sin as a linear basis); keep the (d, ω) with minimum SSE.
Projects: exponentiate the fitted log-curve at t.
5. LPPL — Log-Periodic Power Law (Sornette)
P(t) = A + B·(t_c − t)^m + C·(t_c − t)^m · cos( ω·ln(t_c − t) − φ )
Fit: grid search over the nonlinear params (t_c, m, ω); for each triple the linear params (A, B, and C·cosφ, C·sinφ) are solved by least squares; keep minimum SSE.
Projects: t_c (the critical time) IS the forecast — the projected turning point/date. The plotted price is the curve's value as t → t_c (it accelerates/oscillates near the singularity).
6. Ornstein–Uhlenbeck — mean reversion on log-price (x = ln P)
dx = θ·(μ − x)·dt + σ·dW
Fit: discrete AR(1) x_{t+1} = c + φ·x_t by OLS; then θ = −ln(φ)/Δt, μ = c/(1 − φ), Δt = mean bar spacing.
Forecast: E[x_T] = μ + (x₀ − μ)·e^(−θ·(T − t₀)); P = e^(E[x_T]). μ is the equilibrium (fair value) it reverts toward.
7. Monte Carlo — Merton jump-diffusion (the shaded fan)
S_{t+1} = S_t · exp[ (k − ½σ²)·Δt + σ·√Δt·z + J ], z ~ N(0, 1)
Jumps per step: N ~ Poisson(λ·Δt), J = −0.05·N + 0.12·√N·z′ (only when N > 0), λ = 0.7
Calibration: k = the exponential-fit drift; σ = std(Δln P / √Δt), the per-√month log-volatility; Δt ≈ 0.25 month per step.
Output: 120,000 simulated paths → the P10 / P50 / P90 percentiles per date (median line + shaded 80% band).
8. Regime odds — classification pipeline (Regime tab)
label = composite_classify( return_eff, range_eff, efficiency, ADX )
return_eff = Δnet / (ATR·period) range_eff = (H−L) / (ATR·period) efficiency = |Δnet| / Σ|Δclose| ADX = Wilder(14)
thresholds: {return 0.05, range 0.03, ADX 25, efficiency 0.5}
Paths: empirical bootstrap of demeaned daily log-returns + daily drift kᵈ: r_t = (sampled day's demeaned return) + kᵈ. OHLC synthesized from the sampled day's relative range & wick split, then run through the project composite classifier over a 14-bar window.
Output: N = 2,000 paths → regime probability per month. A GARCH(1,1) filtered-historical-simulation variant (σ²_t = ω + α·e²_{t−1} + β·σ²_{t−1}, α ≈ 0.15, β ≈ 0.71; innovations bootstrapped from standardized residuals) was also run and gave a near-identical mix.

Models: linear, exponential (log-linear), power law (long-run, published coeffs), damped log-oscillation, log-periodic power law (LPPL/Sornette), Ornstein–Uhlenbeck mean-reversion, Monte-Carlo Merton GBM+jumps. Not investment advice — illustrative curve-fitting on a short, noisy window.
